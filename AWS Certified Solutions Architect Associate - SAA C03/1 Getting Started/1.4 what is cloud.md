# ☁️ What is the Cloud?

---

## ⚡ Analogy: Electricity Revolution

### 🕰️ 100 Years Ago
- Large enterprises built their own **power plants** near factories
- Managed electricity generation themselves

---

### 🏙️ Today
- We don’t build power plants anymore
- We simply:
  - Plug devices into sockets
  - Consume electricity from the **power grid**

---

## 💡 Key Insight

> Cloud computing brings the **same revolution to IT infrastructure** as electricity did to power.

---

## ❓ Key Question

> Why buy infrastructure when you can rent it?

---

# ☁️ What is Cloud Computing?

> Cloud = Renting IT infrastructure (servers, storage, databases) over the internet

---

## 🧠 Simple Definition

> Cloud is **someone else’s infrastructure** that you use when needed.

- You don’t own servers  
- You don’t maintain them  
- You just use them  

---

# 🔑 Core Concepts of Cloud

---

## ⚡ 1. On-Demand Access

- Instantly get resources when needed:
  - Servers
  - Storage
  - Databases

✅ No waiting for weeks/months  
✅ Provision in minutes  

---

## 📈 2. Elasticity

> Scale resources up or down based on demand

- Increase servers during high traffic  
- Decrease when demand is low  

✅ No over-provisioning  
✅ No under-provisioning  

---

## 💵 3. Pay-as-you-go

- Pay only for what you use
- No upfront investment

✅ Similar to electricity billing  
✅ Cost-efficient  

---

# 📌 Key Takeaway

> Cloud transforms IT infrastructure into a **utility service**:
- Just like electricity ⚡
- Available on demand
- Paid based on usage
